Dream Catcher's Journeys Throughout the Years :


  • 2017 - Dream Catcher made their official debut on January 13, 2017 formed by Happyface Entertainment (Now Dreamcatcher Company) with single album Nightmare. April 5th, they released second single album Fall Asleep In the Mirror. July 27th, they released their first EP album Fly High.The album debuted at number five on the Billboard World Albums Chart and peaked at number one on the US iTunes K-Pop Top 100 Chart.
  • 2018 - Dream Catcher released their first anniversary digital single Full Moon. In March 2018, the group announced their official fandom name to be InSomnia. On May 10, Dream Catcher released their second EP, Escape the Era. On July 15, Dream Catcher signed with Pony Canyon to make their Japanese debut in the fall. On October 5, it was announced that Dreamcatcher would be debut in Japan in November with a Japanese version of their single "What", scheduled for release on November 21.
  • 2019 - On February 13, Dreamcatcher released their fourth EP, The End of Nightmare. On September 11, the group released their first Japanese studio album titled The Beginning of The End Together with the release of Dream Catcher's Korean comeback album Raid of Dream, the music video of their new Korean song Deja Vu was unveiled on September 18. The song was a collaboration with the mobile game 'King's Raid'.
  • 2020 - The first full album Dystopia: The Tree of Language was released on February 18. The album was the first part of the Dystopia series. Their third Japanese single, Endless Night, was released on March 11. On March 12, Dreamcatcher announced support for Handong's participation in the Chinese Idol-producing show Youth With You. On June 16, Dreamcatcher released a teaser for their new song R.o.S.E BLUE, an O.S.T collaboration for the mobile game Girl Cafe Gun. On July 8, Siyeon released the OST track Good Sera for the drama Memorials, her second after her Love & Secret OST collaboration "Two of Us" in 2014. The fifth EP Dystopia: Lose Myself was released on August 17 and became their best selling album to date. On November 20, Dreamcatcher released their fourth Japanese single No More. On November 21, Siyeon released her third OST track No Mind for the drama Get Revenge.
  • 2021 - Present - The third installment of the "Dystopia" series, the group announced their sixth EP Dystopia: Road to Utopia, which was released on January 26, 2021. On July 30, 2021, Dreamcatcher made their comeback with the song "BEcause" and the special EP Summer Holiday. On April 12, 2022, Dreamcatcher released their second full album Apocalypse: Save Us. On April 20, the group earned their first music show win of their career with "Maison" on MBC M's Show Champion. On April 26, Dreamcatcher earned their second music show win on SBS MTV's The Show. On June 4, Dreamcatcher traveled to Barcelona, Spain to perform at the 2022 Primavera Sound Festival.
